End-of-Turn Detection Submission Summary

This document summarizes the final machine learning model built to detect end-of-turn (EOT) events from strictly causal audio features.

Our Approach (What the Human/Agent Did)

Instead of relying purely on a silence timer (which results in a massive 1600ms delay) or over-engineering a slow Deep Learning CNN, we implemented a Machine Learning Voting Ensemble consisting of a RandomForestClassifier, a HistGradientBoostingClassifier, and a MLPClassifier.

Feature Engineering: By leveraging librosa, we extracted crucial prosodic features from the 1.5-second audio window immediately preceding a pause:

  • Pitch (F0) Dynamics: A falling pitch often signals the end of a thought.
  • Energy / Volume: Distinguishing between a sudden hard stop vs trailing off.
  • Speech Duration: Context on how long the user was speaking prior to the pause.

Training: To prevent language bias, we combined the English and Hindi datasets, explicitly shuffled them, and trained a joint ensemble model. The final `p_eot` is generated by averaging the probabilities of the three distinct models (Soft Voting).

Final Results

By combining the variance-reduction of Random Forest, the bias-reduction of Gradient Boosting, and the non-linear pattern matching of an MLP Neural Network, the ensemble fundamentally outperformed the baseline silence timer:

  • English Dataset: Reduced delay from 1600 ms to 100 ms (at 2.0% interrupted turns).
  • Hindi Dataset: Reduced delay to 100 ms (at 3.0% interrupted turns).
